Earthquake in Turkey

On both sides of the border between Turkey and Syria, over several hundred
kilometers, there is the same fear, dread, anxiety and anger. It was twenty hours after the 7.8 magnitude earthquake that occurred on Monday, February 6. In the
district of Pazarcik, near the large town of Gaziantep and about 60 kilometers from the Syrian border, tens of thousands of people continued to wander along the roadsides, or what was left of them, in search of shelter or aid.
